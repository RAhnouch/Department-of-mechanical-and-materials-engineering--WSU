Thermodynamics 1: Course Overview

This course covers the basic principles of thermodynamics, including energy transfer, system analysis, and thermodynamic laws. It forms the foundation for advanced engineering concepts.

Course Chapters

  • Chapter 1: Introduction to Thermodynamics
  • Chapter 2: Energy, Work, and Heat
  • Chapter 3: First Law of Thermodynamics
  • Chapter 4: Properties of Pure Substances
  • Chapter 5: Second Law of Thermodynamics
  • Chapter 6: Entropy
  • Chapter 7: Thermodynamic Cycles

Chapter 1: Introduction to Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the study of energy transformations. In this chapter, you will learn:

  • Definitions: System, Surroundings, and Boundary.
  • Types of Systems: Open, Closed, and Isolated.
  • Control Volume: Analyzing energy and mass transfer.

Chapter 2: Energy, Work, and Heat

This chapter introduces the concept of energy transfer:

  • Work: Energy transfer due to force and motion.
  • Heat: Energy transfer caused by temperature difference.
  • Kinetic and Potential Energy.

Chapter 3: First Law of Thermodynamics

The First Law of Thermodynamics states that energy is conserved. Topics covered:

  • Closed System Energy Balance.
  • Work and Heat Interactions.
  • Applications: Engines and Power Plants.

Chapter 4: Properties of Pure Substances

Learn about:

  • Phase diagrams and transitions.
  • Property Tables: Pressure, Temperature, and Volume.
  • Enthalpy and Internal Energy.

Chapter 5: Second Law of Thermodynamics

The Second Law focuses on:

  • Entropy and Irreversibility.
  • Heat Engines and Refrigerators.
  • Carnot Efficiency.

Chapter 6: Entropy

Entropy measures the disorder of a system. Key topics:

  • Entropy Change Calculations.
  • Reversible and Irreversible Processes.
  • Applications in Engineering Systems.

Chapter 7: Thermodynamic Cycles

Thermodynamic cycles are essential for energy conversion:

  • Power Cycles: Rankine, Brayton, and Otto.
  • Refrigeration Cycles.
  • Efficiency Calculations.
